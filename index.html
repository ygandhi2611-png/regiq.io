How to use RegIQ
From product description to regulatory clarity — in minutes
1
Pick your module
Choose Regulatory Pathway (RA) to identify your submission route and risk score, or QA Readiness to assess your QMS. Run both for a complete picture.
2
Describe your product or situation
Type a plain-English description — what it does, how it works, target market. No jargon required. Be as brief or detailed as you like.
3
Answer a few clarifying questions
RegIQ asks 2–4 targeted questions to accurately classify your product or assess your QMS against the right standard.
4
Get your scored profile
Receive a risk or readiness score (1–100) with breakdown, top risks or gaps, cited regulations, and direct links to primary regulatory sources.
Regulatory Pathway — RA
  • Product classification
  • FDA center assignment (CDER/CDRH/CBER)
  • Pathway probability assessment (510k/PMA/De Novo/BLA…)
  • FDA, EMA, Health Canada, TGA + more
  • Risk score 1–100 with interlinked breakdown
  • Top risks with cited guidance — connected to pathway confidence
QA Readiness — QA
  • QMS standard mapping
  • Readiness score 1–100
  • Gap analysis: CAPA, docs, supplier
  • Top deficiency flags (FDA 483 modelled)
  • GxP requirements by product
  • Links to FDA, ISO, EMA guidance
Regulatory Risk Score — what the bands mean
Low 1–39 Simple or well-established product type. Straightforward submission pathway with predictable timelines.
Moderate 40–69 Standard complexity. Established pathway exists but meaningful evidence, manufacturing, or post-market gaps to address.
High 70–100 Novel, complex, or combination product. Significant regulatory investment required — early authority engagement strongly recommended.
QA Readiness Score — what the bands mean
High 70–100 Well-prepared QMS. Minor items to close before inspection or audit.
Moderate 40–69 Core systems in place but key gaps remain. Address priority items before scheduling audit.
Low 1–39 QMS needs significant development. Not ready for inspection — foundational work required first.
